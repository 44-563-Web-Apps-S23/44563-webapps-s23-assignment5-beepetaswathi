Bambusa

Bambusa vulgaris forms moderately loose clumps and has no thorns. It has lemon-yellow culms (stems) with green stripes and dark green leaves. Stems are not straight, not easy to split, inflexible, thick-walled, and initially strong. The densely tufted culms grow 10-20 m (30-70 ft) high and 4-10 cm (2-4 in) thick.

Enkianthus

Enkianthus is a deciduous shrub or small tree in the Ericaceae (blueberry) family with a narrowly upright habit of layered branches and tufted foliage. The name enkyos is Greek for the word pregnant and anthos means flower. This is in reference to the swollen urn shape of the flowers.

Euphorbia

Euphorbia milii, commonly called crown of thorns, is a woody, succulent shrub that features (a) fleshy, bright green leaves, (b) inconspicuous flowers in clusters subtended by very showy petal-like red or yellow bracts and (c) thick sharp black thorns (to 1/2" long) which cover its water-storing branches and stems.
